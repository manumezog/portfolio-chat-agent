# Manuel Mezo — Professional Summary

## Who He Is

Manuel Mezo is a Spanish engineer and applied AI builder with over eight years of professional experience spanning Amazon, McKinsey & Company, and independent software development. His career has followed an unusual arc: deep operational analytics at Amazon's scale, strategic consulting at McKinsey, and a deliberate pivot in 2024 to independently building production AI systems — LLM agents, computer vision pipelines, voice-first interfaces, and multi-agent simulations. He holds an MSc in Aerospace Engineering from Universidad Carlos III de Madrid and TU Delft (exchange), and dual bachelor's degrees in Aerospace Engineering and Mechanical Engineering from the Universidad de León.

What makes Manuel distinctive is the combination of engineering rigour, large-scale operational experience, and hands-on AI building. He is not a pure researcher or a pure engineer: he understands what a business problem actually costs, has driven millions of dollars in measurable impact from analytics and operational improvements, and now applies the same precision to building AI products.

## Career Arc

Manuel began his career in supply chain and operations analytics at Amazon, where he spent approximately five years in EU External Fulfillment (EF) and Transportation. At Amazon, he operated in senior individual-contributor and quasi-managerial roles: owning the BI data infrastructure for a 185-node, 400-person organisation; running quality improvement programmes across 25+ 3PL sites in Europe; managing a first-ever programme to launch multi-item shipping capabilities at live 3PL warehouses; and later moving into a dedicated Transportation capacity planning and forecasting role covering 80,000+ trucks per week and $80M+ CAPEX decisions. His Amazon roles were characterised by high ambiguity, self-directed scope expansion, and a pattern of identifying problems that others had not defined or resourced, then building solutions without being asked to.

Following Amazon, Manuel spent a period at McKinsey & Company as a consultant focused on supply chain and operations. He led client-facing engagements for a major train manufacturer and a global pharmaceutical manufacturer, delivering capacity planning tools, delay-recovery analyses, and stakeholder workshops under tight timeframes and in politically complex environments. This experience sharpened his ability to communicate analytical findings to senior non-technical audiences and to manage upward influence across organisational boundaries.

Since 2024 Manuel has been independently developing AI systems. He has shipped multiple production projects: an LLM-powered portfolio chat agent using RAG (this system), a multi-agent supply chain simulation with anomaly detection, a WMS with camera-based barcode scanning, a custom YOLOv8 object-tracking pipeline, and voice-first interfaces using Retell.ai and Voiceflow. His approach to AI development mirrors his operational experience: he focuses on production systems with real use cases, not toy demos.
